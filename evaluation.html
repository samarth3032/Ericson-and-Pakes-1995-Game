Instructions

You are the manager of a firm operating in an industry where demand, competition, and productivity are uncertain. Your objective is to maximize the value of the firm over a horizon of at most 20 periods.

At the beginning of each period, you will observe:
  • The current demand level.
  • The current number of competitors.
  • The current productivity level.
You must then decide whether to remain in the market or exit. If you remain in the market, you must choose an investment level between 1 and 5.

Investment affects productivity in the next period but only probabilistically. Higher investment generally increases the likelihood of achieving higher productivity.

Demand can be either 5 or 15. Demand can either be volatile or persistent. The exact transition probabilities are unknown.

The number of competitors varies over time but is always between 3 and 7.

You may exit at any time. If you exit, the game ends immediately and you receive the displayed exit valuation.
